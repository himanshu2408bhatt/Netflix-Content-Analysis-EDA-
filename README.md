# Netflix-Content-Analysis-EDA-
This project performs an **Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)** on the Netflix movie and TV show catalog dataset to uncover content trends, growth patterns, dominant genres, and key production geographies. It provides valuable insights into Netflix's global content strategy.
## Key Objectives
* To clean and pre-process a raw dataset containing Netflix titles.
* To analyze the distribution of content types (Movies vs. TV Shows).
* To identify the top countries producing content and the most frequent genres.
* To visualize the growth of content added to Netflix over time.
* To calculate and visualize key statistics for movie durations and TV show seasons.

## Data Source
[cite_start]The analysis is based on the `netflix_titles.csv` dataset, which contains information about titles available on Netflix up to September 2021[cite: 1700, 1751].

## Technologies Used
* **Python**
* **Libraries:** `pandas`, `numpy`, `matplotlib`, `seaborn`, `plotly`, `scipy`

## Analysis Summary (Key Takeaways)
* **Content Mix:** Movies (approx. 70%) dominate TV Shows (approx. 30%).
* [cite_start]**Geographical Trends:** The United States is the top producer, with India being the second-largest, demonstrating a strong focus on global content acquisition[cite: 2048, 2051].
* [cite_start]**Acquisition Peak:** Content added to the platform peaked in 2019, reflecting a major expansion phase.
* [cite_start]**Genre Focus:** 'International Movies', 'Dramas', and 'Comedies' are the most frequent genres.
* [cite_start]**Content Length:** The average movie is about 100 minutes, and the average TV Show is a short-run series with ~1.7 seasons[cite: 1611, 1612].
